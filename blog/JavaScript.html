After diving into the world of JavaScript here at Dev Bootcamp, I got a good luck at objects, and their similarities to Ruby hashes. Very similar, right? Mostly. Unlike a Ruby hash, a JavaScript object can store a function. That is, a property within a JavaScript object may contain an expression that affects that objects or other objects, depending on the scope. In a Ruby hash, your goal is simply storage of data. You wouldn't be using a key or value within a Ruby Hash to execute more code.

Ruby and JavaScript are very similar in more ways than this, but it is important to keep in mind their simple differences. As these concepts become more clear, I hope to provide future examples of how this will be important to your development project.
